Model Development

Temoa and the Open Energy Outlook

I'm working with teams at Carnegie Mellon University and NC State University to advance the popular open-source energy system model Temoa and a partner project, the Open Energy Outlook.


My work largely focuses on continuously improving the Temoa energy system optimization model by applying the best practices from the fields of mathematical optimization and macro-energy systems. I also collaborate with team members to design, run, and analyze scenarios for the OEO, which "examines U.S. energy futures to inform energy and climate policy efforts by applying the gold standards of policy-focused academic modeling."
